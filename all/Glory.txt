										<span class="fHeader">Interesting fact:</span>
										In 1831, Captain William Driver, a shipmaster from Salem, Massachusetts, left on one of his many world voyages. Friends presented him with a flag of 24 stars. As the banner opened to the ocean breeze, he exclaimed, "Old Glory." He kept his flag for many years, protecting it during the Civil War, until it was flown over the Tennessee capital. His "Old Glory" became a nickname for all American flags.

Popularly, it is frequently confounded with the nimbus. See aureola, nimbus.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">A concentered burst of sunlight through clouds, as after a storm; a sunburst; a luminous glow of reflected light upon clouds.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">Synonyms Fame, Renown, Honor, Glory. Fame is simply report, repute, whereby one is made widely known for what one is, does, etc.; it may be good or bad, and is thus essentially the same as celebrity: as, an evil fame attaches to all traitors. Renown expresses the same idea through the notion that one is named again and again by the same persons and continually by new persons; it may be bad, but is generally good. Fame may be a weak word, but renown is always strong. Honor is the least external of these words, indicating often only a respectful frame of mind toward another: as, to hold one in honor. The word, however, sometimes has the meaning of a wide and excellent fame. It is the only one of the series that means acts or words of tribute. Glory is superlative fame or honor, but not necessarily of wide extent.</span> </li><li><span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">To exult; rejoice: always with in.</span> </li><li><span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">To be boastful; exult arrogantly: always with in.</span> </li><li><span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">To make glorious; glorify; magnify and honor.</span> </li><li><span class="term">glory</span> <span class="exp">To defile; make dirty.</span> </li>
