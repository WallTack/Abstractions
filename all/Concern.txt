			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					F. <span class="wUets">concerner</span>, LL. <span class="wUets">concernere</span>, to regard, concern, fr. L. <span class="wUets">concernere</span>, to mix or mingle together, as in a sieve for separating; <span class="wUets">con-</span>, + <span class="wUets">cernere</span>, to separate, sift, distinguish by the senses, and especially by the eyes, to perceive, see. See <span class="wUer">Certain</span>
