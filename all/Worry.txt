								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					OE. <span class="wUets">worowen</span>, <span class="wUets">wirien</span>, to strangle, AS. <span class="wUets">wyrgan</span>, in <span class="wUets">āwyrgan</span>,; akin to D. <span class="wUets">worgen</span>, <span class="wUets">wurgen</span>, to strangle, OHG. <span class="wUets">wurgen</span>, G. <span class="wUets">würgen</span>, Lith. <span class="wUets">verszti</span>, and perhaps to E. <span class="wUets">wring</span>, 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					A.S. <i>wyrgan</i>, found in compound <i>áwyrgan</i>, to harm; cf. Dut. <i>worgen</i>, Ger. <i>würgen</i>, to choke; A.S. <i>wearg</i>, <i>werg</i>, a wolf.
