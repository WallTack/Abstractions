												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					OE. <span class="wUets">trecherïe</span>, <span class="wUets">trichere</span>, OF. <span class="wUets">trecherie</span>, <span class="wUets">tricherie</span>, F. <span class="wUets">tricherie</span>, trickery, from <span class="wUets">tricher</span>, to cheat, to trick, OF. <span class="wUets">trichier</span>, <span class="wUets">trechier</span>,; probably of Teutonic origin. See <span class="wUer">Trickery</span> <span class="wUer">Trick</span> 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					O. Fr. <i>tricherie</i>—<i>tricher</i>—Teut., Mid. High Ger. <i>trechen</i>, to draw. <i>Trick</i> is a doublet.
