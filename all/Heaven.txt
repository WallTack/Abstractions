									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">The expanse of space surrounding the earth, and appearing above and around us as a great arch or vault, in which the sun, moon, and stars seem to be set; the sky; the firmament; the celestial regions: often used in the plural.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">Sky as typical of climate; a zone or region.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">The celestial abode of immortal beings; the place or state of existence of blessed spirits beyond the sphere of or after departure from the earthly life. In Christian theology heaven is regarded as the region or state of endless happiness enjoyed by angels and faithful departed spirits in the immediate presence of God. The Hebrews supposed three heavens—the air, the starry firmament, and the abode of God. The Cabalists described seven heavens, each rising in happiness above the other, the highest being the abode of God and the most exalted angels. Hence, to be in the seventh heaven is to be supremely happy. The heaven of the Mohammedans is remarkable for the sensual delights it has in store for the faithful. The ancient Greeks and Latins regarded heaven as the abode of the greater gods; and the spirits of the great and good were supposed to find their place of bliss in the Elysian Fields (which see, under Elysian).</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">[capitalized] The Supreme Being; God; Providence.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">plural The celestial powers; heavenly beings.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">Supreme exaltation or felicity; consummate happiness; a state of bliss.</span> </li><li><span class="term">heaven</span> <span class="exp">To place in or as if in heaven; make supremely happy or blessed; beatify.</span> </li>

					<span>Heaven knows</span> - If you ask someone a question and they say this, they have no idea.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Heaven only knows</span> - The answer to a question is not or cannot be known. For example, heaven only knows when the war will end.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Heavenly bodies</span> - The heavenly bodies are the stars.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Move heaven and earth</span> - This expression indicates a person's determined intention of getting a work done in spite of all odds he may face. He will use all and every means to accomplish the target. Example: He moved heaven and earth to get his literary work recognised by the committee of experts.
				</div>
