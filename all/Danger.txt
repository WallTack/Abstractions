									<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					OE. <span class="wUets">danger</span>, <span class="wUets">daunger</span>, power, arrogance, refusal, difficulty, fr. OF. <span class="wUets">dagier</span>, <span class="wUets">dongier</span>,with same meaning), F. <span class="wUets">danger</span>, danger, fr. an assumed LL. <span class="wUets">dominiarium</span>, power, authority, from L. <span class="wUets">dominium</span>, power, property. See <span class="wUer">Dungeon</span> <span class="wUer">Domain</span> <span class="wUer">Dame</span> 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					O. Fr. <i>dangier</i>, absolute power (of a feudal lord), hence power to hurt.—Low L. <i>dominium</i>, feudal authority—L. <i>dominus</i>, a lord. See <b>Dungeon</b>.
