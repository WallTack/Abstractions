								<h3>In poetry:</h3>
				<div class="usageContainer">
											<div class="poemQuote">
							How did his flowing tears condole<br>
As for a brother dead !<br>
And fasting mortified his soul,<br>
While for their life he prayed.
							<div class="poet">"Psalm XXXV: Now Plead My Cause, Almighty God" by <span>Isaac Watts</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							"Alas! young knight," she weeping said,<br>
"Condole my wretched fate;<br>
A childless mother here you see;<br>
A wife without a mate.
							<div class="poet">"Valentine and Ursine" by <span>Anonymous British</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							And on thy ears my words of weak condoling<br>
Must vainly fall<br>
The funeral bell which in thy heart is tolling,<br>
Sounds over all!
							<div class="poet">"To My Friend OnThe Death Of His Sister" by <span>John Greenleaf Whittier</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							What heart can joy, what soul can sing,<br>
While winter over-runs the spring?<br>
I die, yet can't my death condole;<br>
Lord, save a dying, drooping soul.
							<div class="poet">"The Believer's Soliloquy; Especially in Times of Desertion, Temptation, Affliction, &amp;c." by <span>Ralph Erskine</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							Ah me! said I, nor power I seek, nor gain;<br>
Nor urged by hope of fame these toils endure;<br>
A simple youth, that feels a lover's pain,<br>
And from his friend's condolence hopes a cure.
							<div class="poet">"Elegy VII. He Describes His Vision to An Acquaintance" by <span>William Shenstone</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							Ye sliding streams, that pass so gently by,<br>
Winding your waves, and do not faster flee,<br>
Ioy you to hear my Sepha's Elegy?<br>
Or doe you linger to condole with me?<br>
'Tis to condole, since such is my estate,<br>
Your bubling streams do murmur at my fate.
							<div class="poet">"Arcadius and Sepha" by <span>William Bosworth</span></div>
