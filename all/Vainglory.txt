							Vainglory's a worm which the very best action<br>
Will taint, and its soundness eat through;<br>
But to give one's self airs for a small benefaction,<br>
Is folly and vanity too.
							<div class="poet">"Charity" by <span>Charles Lamb</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							You have come back,—how strange—out of the grave;<br>
Its dreams are in your eyes, and still there clings<br>

And a mysterious rust is on these rings—
							<div class="poet">"Resurrection" by <span>Richard Le Gallienne</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							Is this some grand affair of state,<br>
A coronation, or display,<br>
By some vainglorious potentate,--<br>
Or can this concourse mark the day<br>
Of some victorious hero's march<br>
Homeward, through triumphal arch?
							<div class="poet">"Echoes From Galilee." by <span>Alfred Castner King</span></div>
						</div>

							Should somebody one day knock at your door<br>
Announcing he's an emissary of mine,<br>
Never believe him, nor that it is I;<br>
For to knock does not go with my vainglory,<br>
Even at the unreal door of the sky.
