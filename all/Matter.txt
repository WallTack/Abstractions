									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">Sensible substance; that which offers resistance to touch or muscular effort; that which can be moved, strained, broken, comminuted, or otherwise modified, but which cannot be destroyed or produced; that which reacts against forces, is permanent, and preserves its identity under all changes. Matter has three states of aggregation, the solid, the liquid, and the gas eous. See solid, liquid, gas, and ether.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">In philosophy: That which is in itself nothing definite, but is the subject of change and development, and by receiving a form becomes a substance; that out of which anything is made. See form. Matter in this sense (a translation of Aristotle's word ϋλη, originally wood) is termed by the scholastics matter ex qua (out of which), to distinguish it from matter circum quam (concerning which), or the object of any action or power, as well as from matter in qua (in which), or the subject of any attribute.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">Extended substance.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">In the Kantian terminology, that which receives forms; especially, that element of cognition which comes to us from without; that which distinguishes a particular cognition from others; the purely sensuous part, independent of the representations of space and time and of every operation of thought; the content of experience.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">That of which anything is or may be composed; plastic, formative, or formed material of any kind; material: as, the prime matters of textile fabrics (wool, cotton, silk, etc.); the book contains much useless matter.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">Specifically, in printing: Material for work; copy: as, to keep the compositors supplied with matter.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">Type set up; material to be printed from, or that has been printed from and will not again be required: in the former case called distinctively live matter, and in the latter dead matter.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">In a restricted sense, mere effete substance; that which is thrown off by a living body, or which collects in it as the result of disease; pus: as, fecal matter; purulent or suppurative matter (often called simply matter); the discharge of matter from an abscess or a wound.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">The material of thought or expression; a subject or cause of mental operation or manifestation; intellectual basis or ground; theme; topic; source: as, matter for reflection; a matter of joy or grief.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">A subject of or for consideration or action; importance; consequence: used especially in interrogative and negative phrases, sometimes with an ellipsis of the verb.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">Something indefinite as to amount or quantity; a measure, distance, time, or the like, approximately or vaguely stated.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">In law: Statement or allegation: as, the court may strike out scandalous matter from a pleading.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">matter</span> <span class="exp">A proceeding of a special nature, commenced by motion on petition or order to show cause, etc., as distinguished from a formal action by one party against another, commenced by process and seeking judgment: as, the matter of the application of A.
