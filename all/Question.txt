									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Beg the question</span> - In philosophy "to beg the question" is to assume something to be true that has not yet been proved. I have seen the idiom also to mean that a question is crying out to be asked.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Burning question</span> - A burning question is something we all want to know about.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>No question</span> - This idiom means that something is certain or definite.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>No questions asked</span> - If something is to be done and no questions asked, then it doesn't matter what methods are used or what rules are broken to ensure that it gets done.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Pop the question</span> - When someone pops the question, they ask someone to marry them.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Question of time</span> - If something's a question of time, it's certain to happen, though we don't know exactly when.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Searching question</span> - A searching question goes straight to the heart of the subject matter, possibly requiring an answer with a degree of honesty that the other person finds uncomfortable.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Sixty-four-thousand-dollar-question</span> - The sixty-four-thousand-dollar-question is the most important question that can be asked about something.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
