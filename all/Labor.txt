At the age of 10, his father was sent to debtor's prison, his mother forced into menial labor, this brothers and sisters worked in factories, and Dickens himself had to tie and label sacks of lamp black. He would later draw on these experiences in his novels.

								<ul class="meanings">
									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Work done by a human being or an animal; exertion of body or mind, or both, for the accomplishment of an end; effort made to attain useful results, in distinction from exercise for the sake of recreation or amusement.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Specifically, bodily toil; physical exertion for the sake of gain or reward; the use of muscular strength for the satisfaction of wants, in distinction from purely mental exertion and from the productive use of capital. Skilled labor is that employed in arts and handicrafts which have to be learned by apprenticeship or study and practice; unskilled labor is that requiring no preparatory training. Nearly all work of both classes is included in the phrase manual labor.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Work done or to be done; that which requires exertion or effort; a work; a performance; an achievement: as, the twelve labors of Hercules.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">The laboring class; productive work as represented by those devoted to it: as, the claims or rights of labor; the labor -market.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">The pangs and efforts of childbirth; parturition; travail. The first stage of labor is from the beginning to the complete dilatation of the os uteri; the second stage consists in the expulsion of the child, and the third in that of the afterbirth.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">(Sp. pron. lä-bōr′ ). In the quicksilver-mines of California, any place where work has been or is going on; especially, in the plural, those parts of the mine from which ore is being extracted in some quantity; workings.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Synonyms Toil, Drudgery, etc. (see work); effort, pains.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To make a physical or mental effort to accomplish some end; exert the powers of body or mind for the attainment of some result; work; strive. The word often implies painful or strenuous effort.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Specifically, to exert the muscular power of the body for the attainment of some end; engage in physical or manual toil.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To be burdened; be oppressed with difficulties; proceed or act with difficulty: used absolutely, or followed by under or (formerly) of.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To suffer the pangs of childbirth; be in travail.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To move forward heavily and with difficulty; specifically, of a ship, to roll and pitch heavily in a seaway, or in such a manner as to bring a dangerous strain upon the masts, rigging, and hull.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">Synonyms To struggle, plod, drudge, slave, suffer.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To cause to work; exercise.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To work at; specifically, to till; cultivate.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To produce by labor; make or work out with effort; expend labor on; strive for.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To urge; labor with.</span> </li><li><span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">To beat; belabor.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">labor</span> <span class="exp">A Mexican land-measure, equal to 177 acres.</span> </li>
