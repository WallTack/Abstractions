				<div class="usageContainer">
											<div class="poemQuote">

The poor may take their share;<br>

I'll make no more pretence;<br>

							<div class="poet">"Hymn 86" by <span>Isaac Watts</span></div>
						</div>

Can Adam's sons arrive at heav'n;<br>
New works can give us no pretence<br>

							<div class="poet">"Hymn 35  part 1" by <span>Isaac Watts</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							I, too, can play at the vain pretence,<br>
Can feign you dead; while a voice sounds clear<br>
In the inmost depths of my heart: Go hence,<br>
Go, find your friend who is far from here.
							<div class="poet">"The Sequel to a Reminiscence" by <span>Amy Levy</span></div>
						</div>

							In vain! To college halls, to churchmen's bowers<br>
The feigning spirit pierces and o'erpowers;<br>

							<div class="poet">"Pretence.  Part I - Table-Talk" by <span>John Kenyon</span></div>
						</div>

See now—she drowns me with her tears,<br>
With bony fist, now slaps my ears,<br>
And brings me to my senses.
							<div class="poet">"My Mistresses" by <span>Royall Tyler</span></div>
