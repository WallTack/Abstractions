									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">solitude</span> <span class="exp">The state of being alone; a lonely life; loneliness.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">solitude</span> <span class="exp">Remoteness from society; lack or utter want of companionship: applied to place: as, the solitude of a wood or a valley.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">solitude</span> <span class="exp">A lonely, secluded, or unfrequented place; a desert.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">solitude</span> <span class="exp">Synonyms Solitude, Retirement, Seclusion, Loneliness, Lonesomeness. Solitude is the condition of being absolutely alone, whether or not one has been with others, or desires to escape from them: as, the solitude of the Sphinx. Retirement is comparative solitude, produced by retiring, voluntarily or otherwise, from contact which one has had with others. Seclusion is stronger than retirement, implying the shutting out of others from access: after the Restoration Milton for safety's sake kept himself in retirement; indeed, except to a few trusted friends, he was in complete seclusion. Loneliness expresses the uncomfortable feelings, the longing for society, of one who is alone. Lonesomeness may be a lighter kind of loneliness, especially a feeling less spiritual than physical, growing out of the animal instinct for society and the desire of protection, the consciousness of being alone: as, the lonesomeness of a walk through a cemetery at night. Lonesomeness, more often than loneliness, may express the impression made upon the observer.</span> </li>
