									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>An Englishman's home is his castle</span> - (UK) This means that what happens in a person's home or private life is their business and should not be subject to outside interference.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Bring home the bacon</span> - A person who brings home the bacon earns the money that a family live on.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Charity begins at home</span> - This idiom means that family members are more important than anyone else, and should be the focus of a person's efforts.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Drive home</span> - The idiomatic expression 'drive home' means 'reinforce' as in 'The company offered unlimited technical support as a way to drive home the message that customer satisfaction was its highest priority.'
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Feel at home</span> - If you feel relaxed and comfortable somewhere or with someone, you feel at home.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Hit home</span> - If something hits home, it is understood completely and has a strong effect as people accept it even though it is negative.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Home and hearth</span> - 'Home and hearth' is an idiom evoking warmth and security.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Home is where you lay your hat</span> - Wherever you are comfortable and at ease with yourself is your home, regardless where you were born or brought up.('Home is where you lay your head' and 'Home is where you hang your hat' are also used.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Home stretch</span> - The home stretch is the last part of something, like a journey, race or project.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Home sweet home</span> - This is said when one is pleased to be back at one's own home.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Home, James</span> - (UK) This is a cliched way of telling the driver of a vehicle to start driving. It is supposed to be an order to a chauffeur (a privately employed driver). The full phrase is 'Home, James, and don't spare the horses'.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Nothing to write home about</span> - Something that is not special or good is nothing to write home about.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Till the cows come home</span> - This idioms means 'for a very long time'. ('Until the cows come home' is also used.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>When the chickens come home to roost</span> - When a person pays dearly for something bad he or she did in the past, the chickens come home to roost.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
