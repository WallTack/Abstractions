				<div class="etymology">
					OE. <span class="wUets">nigromaunce</span>, <span class="wUets">nigromancie</span>, OF. <span class="wUets">nigromance</span>, F. <span class="wUets">nécromance</span>, <span class="wUets">nécromancie</span>, from L. <span class="wUets">necromantia</span>, Gr. ; <span class="wUgrk">nekro`s</span> a dead body (akin to L. <span class="wUets">necare</span>, to kill, Skr. <span class="wUets">naç</span>, to perish, vanish) +  divination, fr.  diviner, seer, akin to E. <span class="wUets">mania</span>,. See <span class="wUer">Mania</span>, and cf. <span class="wUer">Internecine</span> <span class="wUer">Noxious</span>. The old spelling is due to confusion with L. <span class="wUets">niger</span>, black. Hence the name <span class="wUets">black art</span>,
