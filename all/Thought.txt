									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">The act or the product of thinking. Psychologically considered, thought has two elements—one a series of phenomena of consciousuess during an interval of time in which there is no noticeable interruption of the current of association by outward reactions (peripheral sensations and muscular efforts); the other a more or less definite acquisition to the stock of mental possessions—namely, a notion, which may repeatedly present itself and be recognized as ideutical. The former of these elements is the act of thinking as it appears to consciousness; the latter is the lasting effect produced upon the mind, likewise considered from the point of view of consciousness. In the most concrete sense, a single step in a process of thinking; a notion; a reflection.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">The condition or state of a person during such mental action.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">A synonym of cognition in the common threefold division of modes of consciousness: from the fact that thought, as above described, embraces every cognitive process except sensation, which is a mode of consciousness more allied to volition than to other kinds of cognition.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">The objective element of the intellectual product.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">A judgment or mental proposition, in which form the concept always appears.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">An argument, inference, or process of reasoning, by which process the concept is always produced.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">A concept, considered as something which, under the influence of experience and mental action, has a development of its own, more or less independent of individual caprices, and that in the life of an individual, and in history: as, the gradual development of Greek thought.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">thought</span> <span class="exp">The subjective element of intellectual activity; 									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>A penny for your thoughts</span> - This idiom is used as a way of asking someone what they are thinking about.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Food for thought</span> - If something is food for thought, it is worth thinking about or considering seriously.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Perish the thought</span> - Perish the thought is an expression meaning that you really hope something will not happen.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Second thoughts</span> - If some has second thoughts, they start to think that an idea, etc, is not as good as it sounded at first and are starting to have doubts.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Train of thought</span> - A train of thought is a sequence of thoughts, especially when you are talking to someone and you forget what you were going to say.
				</div>
