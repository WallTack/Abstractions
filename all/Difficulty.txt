												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					L. <span class="wUets">difficultas</span>, fr. <span class="wUets">difficilis</span>, difficult; <span class="wUets">dif-</span>, <span class="wUets">= dis-</span>, + <span class="wUets">facilis</span>, easy: cf. F. <span class="wUets">difficulté</span>,. See <span class="wUer">Facile</span> 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					The adj. was formed from <i>difficulty</i>, in place of the old form <i>difficile</i>. Fr. <i>difficulté</i>—L. <i>difficultas</i> = <i>difficilitas</i>—<i>difficilis</i>—<i>dif</i> (= <i>dis</i>), neg., and <i>facilis</i>, easy.
				</div>
