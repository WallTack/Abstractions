									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Air your dirty laundry in public</span> - If you air your dirty laundry in public, you reveal aspects of your private life that should really remain private, by telling a secret, arguing in public, etc.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Don't wash your dirty laundry in public</span> - (UK) People, especially couples, who argue in front of others or involve others in their personal problems and crises, are said to be washing their dirty laundry in public; making public things that are best left private. (In American English, 'don't air your dirty laundry in public' is used.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>John Q Public</span> - (USA) John Q Public is the typical, average person.
				</div>
