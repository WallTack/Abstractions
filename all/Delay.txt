												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					F. <span class="wUets">délai</span>, fr. OF. <span class="wUets">deleer</span>, to delay, or fr. L. <span class="wUets">dilatum</span>, which, though really from a different root, is used in Latin only as a p. p. neut. of <span class="wUets">differre</span>, to carry apart, defer, delay. See <span class="wUer">Tolerate</span>, and cf. <span class="wUer">Differ</span> <span class="wUer">Delay</span> (v.) 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Fr. <i>délayer</i>, dilute—L. <i>deliquāre</i>, clarify.
