									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Behind closed doors</span> - If something happens away from the public eye, it happens behind closed doors.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close at hand</span> - If something is close at hand, it is nearby or conveniently located.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close but no cigar</span> - (USA) If you are close but no cigar, you are close to success or the truth, but have not got there.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close call</span> - If the result of something is a close call, it is almost impossible to distinguish between the parties involved and to say who has won or whatever. It can also mean that you very nearly have a serious accident or get into trouble.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close lipped</span> - A person who is reluctant to talk about a specific subject is close lipped.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close only counts in horseshoes and hand grenades</span> - This phrase is used to say that if you come close to success without succeeding, it is not good enough
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close ranks</span> - If members of an organisation close ranks, they show support for each other publicly, especially when being criticised. It is a military term- when soldiers close ranks, they stand closer together so that it is difficult to pass through them.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close shave</span> - If you have a close shave, you very nearly have a serious accident or get into trouble.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close the book</span> - If you close the book on something, you end it completely.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close the stable door after the horse has bolted</span> - If people try to fix something after the problem has occurred, they are trying to close the stable door after the horse has bolted. 'Close the barn door after the horse has bolted' is alternative, often used in American English.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Close to your heart</span> - If something is close to your heart, you care a lot about it. ('Dear to your heart' is an alternative.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Closed book to me</span> - If a subject is a closed book to you, it is something that you don't understand or know anything about.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Sail close to the wind</span> - If you sail close to the wind, you take risks to do something, going close to the limit of what is allowed or acceptable.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
