												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					It is said that Daly, the manager of a Dublin playhouse, laid a wager that a new word of no meaning should be the common talk and puzzle of the city in twenty-four hours. In consequence of this the letters <span class="wUets">q u i z</span>, were chalked by him on all the walls of Dublin, with an effect that won the wager. Perhaps, however, originally a variant of <span class="wUets">whiz</span>, and formerly the name of a popular game
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Origin obscure; doubtless framed from <i>question</i>, or direct from L. <i>quæso</i>, I ask.
