												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Cf. F. <span class="wUets">bigoterie</span>, 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					O. Fr.; of dub. origin; variously conn. with <i>Visigoth</i>, they being Arians, while the Franks were orthodox; with Sp. <i>bigote</i>, a moustache; with <i>Beguine</i> (q.v.); and by Wace with a worthless legend that the Norman Rollo, in refusing to kiss the foot of Charles the Simple, said, 'Ne se, <i>bi got</i>.'
