				<div class="etymology">
					L., from Gr. <span class="wUgrk">pa`qos</span> a suffering, passion, fr. <span class="wUgrk">paqei^n</span> <span class="wUgrk">pas`chein</span>, to suffer; cf. <span class="wUgrk">po`nos</span> toil, L. <span class="wUets">pati</span>, to suffer, E. <span class="wUets">patient</span>,

				<div class="usageContainer">
											<div class="poemQuote">

Of many a friend, who yet conceals<br>

						</div>

							<div class="poet">"The Land Of Illusion" by <span>Madison Julius Cawein</span></div>

With trembling accents low,<br>
The plaintive breezes of the vale,<br>

And grief, in every face, appears.
							<div class="poet">"To Eloquence" by <span>James Avis Bartley</span></div>

Hers is the holy calm of some meek saint.
