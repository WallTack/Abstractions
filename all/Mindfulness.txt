									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Blow your mind</span> - Something that will blow your mind is something extraordinary that will amaze you beyond explanation.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Cast your mind back</span> - If somebody tells you to cast your mind back on something, they want you to think about something that happened in the past, but which you might not remember very well, and to try to remember as much as possible.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Give someone a piece of your mind</span> - If you give someone a piece of your mind, you criticise them strongly and angrily.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Great minds think alike</span> - If two people have the same thought at the same time, one of them might say "Great minds think alike."
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Half a mind</span> - If you have half a mind to do something, you haven't decided to do it, but are thinking seriously about doing it.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>In two minds</span> - If you are in two minds about something, you can't decide what to do.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Mind over matter</span> - This idiom is used when someone uses their willpower to rise above adversity.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Mind the gap</span> - Mind the gap is an instruction used on the Underground in the UK to warn passengers to be careful when leaving the tube or train as there is quite a distance between the train and the platform.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Mind your own beeswax</span> - (USA) This idiom means that people should mind their own business and not interfere in other people's affairs.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Mind Your P's and Q's</span> - If you are careful about the way you behave and are polite, you mind Your P's and Q's.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Out of sight, out of mind</span> - Out of sight, out of mind is used to suggest that someone will not think or worry about something if it isn't directly visible or available to them.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Out of your mind</span> - If someone is out of the mind, they are so emotional about something that they are no longer rational.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Presence of mind</span> - If someone behaves calmly and rationally in difficult circumstances, they show presence of mind.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Put you in mind</span> - If something suggests something to you, it puts you in mind of that thing.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Speak your mind</span> - If you speak your mind, you say what you exactly think about a subject regardless of whether others will agree with you or not.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
