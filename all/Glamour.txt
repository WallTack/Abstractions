												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Scot. <span class="wUets">glamour</span>, <span class="wUets">glamer</span>,; cf. Icel. <span class="wUets">glámeggdr</span>, one who is troubled with the glaucoma (?); or Icel. <span class="wUets">glām-sȳni</span>, weakness of sight, glamour; <span class="wUets">glāmr</span>, name of the moon, also of a ghost + <span class="wUets">sȳni</span>, sight, akin to E. <span class="wUets">see</span>,. Perh., however, a corruption of E. <span class="wUets">gramarye</span>, 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Merely a corruption of <i>gramarye</i> or <i>grammar</i>, meaning grammar, then magic.
				</div>
