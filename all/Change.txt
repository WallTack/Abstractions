			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Change horses in midstream</span> - If people change horses in midstream, they change plans or leaders when they are in the middle of something, even though it may be very risky to do so.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Change of heart</span> - If you change the way you think or feel about something, you have a change of heart.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Change tack</span> - If you change tack, you use a different method for dealing with something.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Change your tune</span> - If someone changes their ideas or the way they talk about them, they change their tune.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Chop and change</span> - If things chop and change, they keep changing, often unexpectedly.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Leopard can't change its spots</span> - This idiom means that people cannot change basic aspects of their character, especially negative ones. ("A leopard doesn't change its spots" is also used.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Sea change</span> - An expression that connotes big change; a significant change in comparison to a minor, trivial or insignificant change.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Short-change</span> - If you are short-changed, someone cheats you of money or doesn't give you full value for something.
