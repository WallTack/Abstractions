									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Ahead of time</span> - If something happens ahead of time, it happens early or before the set time.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Been around the block a few times</span> - Someone who says they've been around the block a few times is indicating that they have life experience relating to the topic at hand. It is not necessary to discuss the introductory aspects of the topic or give beginner level advice.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Behind the times</span> - Someone that is behind the times is old-fashioned and has ideas that are regarded as out-dated.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Big time</span> - This can be used to with the meaning 'very much'- if you like something big time, you like it a lot.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Call time</span> - (UK) If you call time on something, you decide it is time to end it.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Crunch time</span> - When people, companies, etc, have to make an important decision that will have a considerable effect on their future, it is crunch time.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Do time</span> - (UK) When someone is doing time, they are in prison.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>For the time being</span> - For the time being indicates that an action or state will continue into the future, but is temporary. I'm sharing an office for the time being.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fullness of time</span> - If something happens in the fullness of time, it will happen when the time is right and appropriate.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Good time</span> - If you make good time on a journey, you manage to travel faster than you expected.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>If I had a nickel for every time</span> - (USA) When someone uses this expression, they mean that the specific thing happens a lot. It is an abbreviation of the statement 'If I had a nickel for every time that happened, I would be rich'
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Just in the nick of time</span> - If you do something in the nick of time, you just manage to do it just in time, with seconds to spare.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Long time no hear</span> - The speaker could say this when they have not heard from a person, either through phone calls or emails for a long time.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Long time no see</span> - 'Long time no see' means that the speaker has not seen that person for a long time.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Nick of time</span> - If you do something in the nick of time, you do it at the very last minute or second.
