			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					F. <span class="wUets">vengeance</span>, fr. <span class="wUets">venger</span>, to avenge, L. <span class="wUets">vindicare</span>, to lay claim to, defend, avenge, fr. <span class="wUets">vindex</span>, a claimant, defender, avenger, the first part of which is of uncertain origin, and the last part akin to <span class="wUets">dicere</span>, to say. See <span class="wUer">Diction</span>, and cf. <span class="wUer">Avenge</span> <span class="wUer">Revenge</span> <span class="wUer">Vindicate</span> 
				</div>

				<div class="etymology">
					O. Fr. <i>venger</i>—L. <i>vindicāre</i>.
