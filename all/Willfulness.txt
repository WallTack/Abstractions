									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Barkus is willing</span> - This idiom means that someone is willing to get married.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Black will take no other hue</span> - Evil can take many disguises but it is always black (evil).
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Boys will be boys</span> - Boys will be boys means that boys, or men, will behave in certain ways, often noisily or irresponsibily.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Heads will roll</span> - If heads will roll, people will be punished or sacked for something that has gone wrong.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>If you lie down with dogs, you will get up with fleas</span> - This means that if you become involved with bad company, there will be negative consequences.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>If you lie down with the Devil, you will wake up in hell</span> - This means that if you become involved with bad company, there will be negative consequences.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>If you will</span> - 'If you will' is used as a way of making a concession in a sentence: He wasn't a very honest person, a liar if you will. Here, it is used a way of accepting that the reader or listener might think of the person as a liar, but without commit the writer or speaker to that position fully.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Look after the pennies and the pounds will look after themselves</span> - (UK) If you look after the pennies, the pounds will look after themselves, meaning that if someone takes care not to waste small amounts of money, they will accumulate capital. ('Look after the pence and the pounds will look after themselves' is an alternative form of this idiom.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Lord willing and the creek don't rise</span> - Pertains to the ability to accomplish a task or meet an obligation, barring unforseen complications. Example: "I will be at work tomorrow, Lord willing and the creek don't rise."
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Murder will out</span> - This idiom means that bad deeds can't be kept secret forever.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>None so blind as those who will not see</span> - This idiom is used when people refuse to accept facts presented to them. ('None so deaf as those who will not hear' is an alternative.)
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Spirit is willing, but the flesh is weak</span> - If the spirit is willing but the flesh is weak, someone lacks the willpower to change things they do because they derive too much pleasure from them.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Sticks and stones may break my bones, but names will never hurt me</span> - To be resistant to criticism. This is often said to young children upset over the fact that another child called them something that they did not like.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>That and 50 cents will buy you a cup of coffee</span> - (USA) This is used to describe something that is deemed worthless. "He's got a Ph.D. in Philosophy." "So? That and 50 cents will buy you a cup of coffee."
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Truth will out</span> - Truth will out means that, given time, the facts of a case will emerge no matter how people might try to conceal them.
				</div>
