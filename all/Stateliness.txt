							<div class="poet">"The Mystery Of A Year" by <span>Archibald Lampman</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							The uncurbed rivulets raced to where<br>

Shot through the wave without a sound.
							<div class="poet">"The Door Of Humility" by <span>Alfred Austin</span></div>
						</div>

							From Venice, while the moonlight falls<br>
Brightest o'er her porphyry halls,<br>
Gilding with unearthly ray,<br>
Dome and column, worn and grey,<br>
Till, as in a fairy dream,<br>
Prouder, statelier, they seem
							<div class="poet">"Gondolier's Song: In An Unfinished Mask" by <span>Sir John Hanmer</span></div>
						</div>

There is not a nobler, statelier pile<br>

Though hollowed by time, are not as old<br>
