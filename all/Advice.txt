												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					OE. <span class="wUets">avis</span>, F. <span class="wUets">avis</span>,; <span class="wUets">, + OF. <ets>vis</ets></span>, fr. L. <span class="wUets">visum</span>, seemed, seen; really p. p. of <span class="wUets">videre</span>, to see, so that <span class="wUets">vis</span>, meant that which has seemed best. See <span class="wUer">Vision</span>, and cf. <span class="wUer">Avise</span> <span class="wUer">Advise</span> 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					O. Fr. <i>advis</i> (Fr. <i>avis</i>)—L. <i>ad visum</i>, according to what is seen or seems best.
