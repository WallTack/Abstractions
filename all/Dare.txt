									<li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To be bold enough (to do something); have courage, strength of mind, or hardihood (to undertake some action or project); not to be afraid; venture: followed by an infinitive (with or without to) as object, or sometimes, by ellipsis, used absolutely.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">[Originally and still often used in the third person of the present tense without a personal termination, and in such case always followed by the infinitive without to: as, he dare not do it.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To venture on; attempt boldly to perform.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To challenge; provoke to action, especially by asserting or implying that one lacks courage to accept the challenge; defy: as, to dare a man to fight.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To arouse; rouse.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">The quality of being daring; venturesomeness; boldness; dash; spirit.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">A challenge; defiance.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To be in fear; tremble with fear; be stupefied or dazed with fear. Specifically</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To lie still in fear; lurk in dread; especially, lie or squat close to the ground, like a frightened bird or hare; look anxiously around, as such a lurking creature.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To droop; languish.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To strike with fear; terrify; daunt; dismay.</span> </li><li><span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">To terrify and catch (larks), as by means of a mirror or a piece of red cloth, or by walking round with a hawk on the fist where they are crouching, and then throwing a net over them.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">A mirror for daring larks.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">Same as dace, 1.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">dare</span> <span class="exp">A Middle English form of deer.</span> </li>
