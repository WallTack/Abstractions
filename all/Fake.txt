												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Cf. Scot. <span class="wUets">faik</span>, fold, stratum of stone, AS. <span class="wUets">fæc</span>, space, interval, G. <span class="wUets">fach</span>, compartment, partition, row, and E. <span class="wUets">fay</span>, to fit
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					Prof. Skeat thinks it merely the Mid. Dut. <i>facken</i>, to catch; Mr Bradley suggests the earlier <i>feak</i>, <i>feague</i>, Ger. <i>fegen</i>, to furbish up.
