									<h2 id="_idioms" class="header">Idioms</h2>
			<div id="idioms" class="subContainer">
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>All's fair in love and war</span> - This idiom is used to say that where there is conflict, people can be expected to behave in a more vicious way.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Faint heart never won fair lady</span> - This means that you will not get the partner of your dreams if you lack the confidence to let them know how you feel.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fair and square</span> - If someone does something fair and square, they do it correctly, following any rules or laws.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fair crack of the whip</span> - (UK) If everybody has a fair crack of the whip, they all have equal opportunities to do something.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fair game</span> - If something or someone is fair game, then it is acceptable to target, criticise or attack them.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fair shake of the whip</span> - (USA) If everybody has a fair shake of the whip, they all have equal opportunities to do something.
				</div>	
				<span class="separatorSmall">***</span>
								<div class="idioms">
					<span>Fair thee well</span> - Meaning completely and fully: I am tied up today to a fair-thee-well.
				</div>
