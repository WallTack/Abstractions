							Next night she died; and her obsequies<br>
In the Field of Tombs, by the Via renowned,<br>
Had her husband's heed. His tendance spent,<br>
I often went<br>
And pondered by her mound.

											<div class="poemQuote">
							Hark! how the Orient's bells are proclaiming<br>
Obsequies strange to the shrines of the west--<br>
Services Christendom's cruelties shaming--<br>

							<div class="poet">"The Unforgotten Heroes" by <span>John Lawson Stoddard</span></div>
						</div>

							I saw th' obsequious seraphim<br>
Their rosy fleece of fire bestow,<br>
For well they now can spare their wings,<br>
Since Heaven itself lies here below.<br>
Well done, said I; but are you sure<br>
Your down, so warm, will pass for pure?
							<div class="poet">"Verses From The Shepherds' Hymn" by <span>Richard Crashaw</span></div>
						</div>

							I'll not attend the obsequies: I feel I could not face<br>

We saw a generation pass while side by side we sat:<br>
Another starts its march to-day-without us, Father Pat.
							<div class="poet">"Vale`  - Father Pat" by <span>John O Brien</span></div>
						</div>
