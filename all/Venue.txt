												<h2 id="_etymology" class="header">Etymology</h2>
			<div id="etymology" class="subContainer">
								<div class="dName">Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					F. <span class="wUets">venue</span>, a coming, arrival, fr. <span class="wUets">venir</span>, to come, L. <span class="wUets">venire</span>,; hence, in English, the place whither the jury are summoned to come. See <span class="wUer">Come</span>, and cf. <span class="wUer">Venew</span> <span class="wUer">Veney</span> 
				</div>	
								<div class="dName">Chambers's Twentieth Century Dictionary</div>
				<div class="etymology">
					A particular use of preceding word, but confused with O. Fr. <i>visne</i>, neighbourhood—L. <i>vicinia</i>, neighbourhood.
