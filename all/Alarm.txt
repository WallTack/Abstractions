									<li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">A summons to arms, as on the approach of an enemy; hence, any sound, outcry, or information intended to give notice of approaching danger.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">A hostile attack; a tumult; a broil; a disturbance.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">A sudden fear or painful suspense excited by an apprehension of danger; apprehension; fright: as, there is nothing in his illness to cause alarm.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">A warning sound; a signal for attention; an urgent call, summons, or notification. Specifically— In fencing, an appeal or a challenge made by a step or stamp on the ground with the advancing foot.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">A self-acting contrivance of any kind used to call attention, rouse from sleep, warn of danger, etc. Such devices are made in a great variety of forms, as, for example, alarm-clocks, fog-bells, fog-whistles, and sounding or whistling buoys; bells to indicate changes in temperature, the opening or shutting of doors, gates, or drawers, the arrival of a given hour, or the condition of telephone- and telegraph-wires; signals to call attention to the escape of gas, steam, water, air, etc.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Alarm, Apprehension, Fright, Terror, Dismay, Consternation, Panic, affright, agitation, flutter, perturbation. These words all express degrees of fear in view of possible or certain, perhaps imminent, danger.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Apprehension is the lowest degree of fear; the mind takes hold of the idea of danger, and without alarm considers the best way of meeting it.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Alarm is the next stage; by derivation it is the alarum or summons to arms. The feelings are agitated in view of sudden or just-discovered danger to one's self or others. Generally its effect upon the mind is like that of apprehension; it energizes rather than overpowers the mental faculties.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Fright, terror, and dismay are higher and perhaps equal degrees of fear; their difference is in kind and in effect.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Fright affects especially the nerves and senses, being generally the effect of sudden fear.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Terror may be a later form of fright, or independent and as sudden; it overpowers the understanding and unmans one.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Dismay appals or breaks down the courage and hope, and therefore, as suggested by its derivation, the disposition to do anything to ward off the peril; what dismays one may be the failure or loss of his chosen means of defense.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Fright and terror are often the effect of undefined fears, as in superstition, and are especially used with reference to physical fear.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Consternation overwhelms the mental faculties by the suddenness or the utterly unexpected greatness of the danger.</span> </li><li><span class="tSpeech">n</span> <span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">Panic is a peculiar form of fear; it is sudden, demoralizing, a temporary madness of fear, altogether out of proportion to its cause; there may even be no cause discoverable. It is the fear of a mass of people, or, figuratively, of animals.</span> </li><li><span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">To call to arms for defense; give notice of danger to; rouse to vigilance and exertions for safety: as, alarm the watch.</span> </li><li><span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">To surprise with apprehension of danger; disturb with sudden fear; fill with anxiety by the prospect of evil.</span> </li><li><span class="term">alarm</span> <span class="exp">To give an alarm.</span> </li>
