				<div class="usageContainer">
											<div class="poemQuote">
							The quick compunction cannot serve;<br>
She saw the flash,<br>
Ere he had bent with busy hand<br>
And drooping lash.
							<div class="poet">"The Selector’s Wife" by <span>Mary Eliza Fullerton</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							He neither would, or could repent,<br>
No true compunction felt;<br>
'Till God in mercy Nathan sent,<br>
His stubborn heart to melt. 
							<div class="poet">"David's Fall" by <span>John Newton</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							Compunction weeps our guilt away,<br>
The sinner's safety is his pain;<br>
Such pangs for our offences pay,<br>
And these severer griefs are gain.
							<div class="poet">"The Hall Of Justice" by <span>George Crabbe</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							And I did. And I gave the child my love,<br>
And the child loved me, and estranged us none.<br>
But compunctions loomed; for I'd harmed the dead<br>
By what I'd said<br>
For the good of the living one.
							<div class="poet">"Her Death And After" by <span>Thomas Hardy</span></div>
						</div>
											<div class="poemQuote">
							How Love then exulted, and laughed at his pain;<br>
He laughed, lost his breath, fell a-laughing again,<br>
Till, feeling a little compunction of heart,<br>
He called Father Hymen to draw out the dart.
							<div class="poet">"Songs" by <span>Peter John Allan</span></div>
						</div>

For tender recollections, cherished long,<br>
For guileless griefs, which no compunction stains,<br>
We blush; as if we wore these earthly chains<br>
Only for sport and song!
